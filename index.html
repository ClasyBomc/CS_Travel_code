Our Grand Indian Places

Jaipur: The Pink City

Explore Jaipur, where buildings are pink, forts are grand, and markets are full of life. It's a city alive with history!

  • Amber Fort (a huge old fort!)
  • Hawa Mahal (Wind Palace, with many windows)
  • City Palace (where kings used to live)

Agra: City of the Taj

Visit Agra, home of the beautiful Taj Mahal. There are also many old forts and tombs with amazing stories.

  • Taj Mahal (the famous white building)
  • Agra Fort (another big fort)
  • Fatehpur Sikri (an old, empty city)

Varanasi: Holy City

Varanasi is a very old and special city by the holy Ganges River. See ancient temples and unique ceremonies here.

  • Ganges River Banks (Ghats)
  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple (an important temple)
  • Sarnath (where Buddha taught)

Jodhpur: The Blue City

Discover Jodhpur, famous for its blue houses and the enormous Mehrangarh Fort. Walk through its blue streets and lively markets.

  • Mehrangarh Fort (a giant fort)
  • Jaswant Thada (a beautiful marble memorial)
  • Ghanta Ghar (the Clock Tower)

Goa: Beaches & Heritage

Relax on golden beaches, explore charming old churches, and experience the lively nightlife of India's favorite coastal state. A blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures awaits!

  • Calangute Beach
  • Old Goa Churches
  • Anjuna Flea Market

Mumbai: City of Dreams

Experience the energetic pulse of India's financial and entertainment capital. Enjoy the iconic Marine Drive, bustling markets, and historical landmarks by the Arabian Sea.

  • Gateway of India
  • Marine Drive
  • Elephanta Caves

Explore Jaipur: The Pink City

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is a mix of old history and new city life. Its special pink buildings, lively markets, and grand forts and palaces make you feel like you've gone back in time to when kings and queens ruled.

The majestic Amber Fort, a blend of Hindu-Mughal architecture, known for its artistic style elements and stunning views.

The iconic 'Palace of Winds' with its unique five-story exterior, built for royal ladies to observe street festivals.

A grand complex of palaces, gardens, and courtyards, reflecting a fusion of Rajput, Mughal, and European styles.

A beautiful palace located in the middle of Man Sagar Lake, appearing to float on the water.

The oldest museum in Rajasthan, housing a rich collection of artifacts including paintings, carpets, and jewelry.

Vibrant and bustling markets like Johari Bazaar, famous for jewelry, textiles, and traditional crafts.

From the tiny windows of Hawa Mahal to the big open spaces of City Palace, Jaipur is amazing to look at. Don't forget to visit the colorful markets to buy traditional clothes, jewelry, and handmade items.

Explore Agra: City of the Taj Mahal

Agra, by the Yamuna River, is famous for the incredible Taj Mahal, a world wonder and a sign of forever love. But there's more to Agra than just the Taj Mahal, with other grand old buildings.

The magnificent white marble mausoleum, a symbol of eternal love and a UNESCO World Heritage site.

A powerful fortress made of red sandstone, which served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors.

A perfectly preserved ghost city built by Emperor Akbar, known for its grand buildings and unique architecture.

A charbagh (four-part garden) complex across the Yamuna River, offering a perfect view of the Taj Mahal.

Often called the 'Baby Taj', this tomb is a delicate marble masterpiece preceding the Taj Mahal.

Agra is famous for marble inlay work, leather goods, and other traditional handicrafts.

Visit the grand Agra Fort and the old, quiet city of Fatehpur Sikri. Both tell stories of Mughal emperors and their amazing buildings.

Explore Varanasi: Holy Heart of India

Varanasi, also called Kashi, is one of the oldest cities where people still live today. It's a very spiritual place by the holy Ganges River, offering a special cultural and religious experience.

Witness the spiritual dawn as devotees gather for rituals on the sacred Ganges River banks.

The captivating evening ceremony where priests offer prayers with lamps to the holy River Ganges.

One of the most famous Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, a central part of Varanasi's spiritual identity.

A significant Buddhist site where Lord Buddha first taught the Dharma after attaining enlightenment.

Watch the beautiful Ganga Aarti ceremony, take a boat ride as the sun rises, and walk through the winding streets filled with old temples and lively local life. You should also visit Sarnath, where Buddha first taught his ideas.

Explore Jodhpur: The Blue City

Jodhpur, with its famous blue-painted houses, stands proudly at the edge of the Thar Desert. With the huge Mehrangarh Fort watching over it, this 'Blue City' gives you a taste of Rajasthan's desert charm and rich history.

A colossal fortress perched on a hilltop, offering panoramic views of the Blue City and housing impressive palaces.

A beautiful marble cenotaph built in memory of Maharaja Jaswant Singh II, known for its intricate carvings.

A grand royal palace, part hotel, part museum, and part royal residence, an architectural marvel.

Wander through the charming narrow alleys of the old city, painted in distinctive blue hues.

The lively Sardar Market surrounding the iconic Ghanta Ghar (Clock Tower), famous for spices and handicrafts.

Explore the giant Mehrangarh Fort for amazing views, visit the peaceful Jaswant Thada, and wander through the busy Sardar Market near the Clock Tower. Jodhpur's blue alleys are perfect for taking beautiful pictures!

Explore Goa: Beaches & Heritage

Goa is India's smallest state but a giant when it comes to tourism! Famous for its stunning coastline, from peaceful coves to bustling party spots, it also boasts a rich history evident in its charming churches and colonial architecture.

One of Goa's most popular and bustling beaches, known for its lively atmosphere and water sports.

A UNESCO World Heritage site in Old Goa, famous for housing the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier.

A vibrant Wednesday market offering everything from handicrafts and jewelry to clothes and souvenirs.

A spectacular four-tiered waterfall on the Mandovi River, resembling a 'sea of milk'.

A beautiful crescent-shaped beach in South Goa, known for its calm waters and relaxed vibe.

Savor the unique flavors of Goan food, a delicious blend of Indian, Portuguese, and Konkani influences.

Whether you're looking for sun-soaked relaxation, thrilling water sports, or a taste of unique Indo-Portuguese culture and cuisine, Goa has it all. Don't miss the vibrant markets and delicious seafood!

Explore Mumbai: The City of Dreams

Mumbai, India's bustling financial and entertainment hub, is a city that never sleeps. From colonial architecture and vibrant street life to the glamour of Bollywood and serene coastal promenades, Mumbai offers a diverse and unforgettable experience.

Mumbai's most famous monument, a grand arch overlooking the Arabian Sea, built to commemorate King George V's visit.

Also known as 'The Queen's Necklace,' this beautiful promenade along the Arabian Sea is perfect for evening strolls.

A historic railway station and UNESCO World Heritage site, a stunning example of Victorian Gothic architecture.

The world's largest open-air laundromat, where clothes are washed in large open concrete wash pens.

An impressive cable-stayed bridge connecting Bandra with Worli, offering panoramic city and sea views.

The heart of the Indian film industry, visit film city or a studio to get a glimpse of moviemaking.

Take a stroll along the iconic Marine Drive, visit the historic Gateway of India, or explore the bustling markets and vibrant cultural scene. Mumbai's energy is truly infectious.
